CONSENT FORM

Consumer Decision Making

Introduction

You are invited to take part in an academic research study being conducted by researchers at Erasmus University Rotterdam and University College of London. Your participation in this study is voluntary and you may withdraw from the study at any time. The study is described as below. Participating in the study might not benefit you, but we might learn things that will benefit others.

Purpose of the study

The purpose of this study is to understand how consumers make decisions on purchasing products.

Who can participate in the study

You can participate in the study if you are an adult registered with MTurk Systems.

Note: In order for you to get through the study smoothly, it is important that you have a full computer or laptop display. It would be highly inconvenient and frustrating for you to attempt to do the study on a device with a small display (example: smartphones and tablets).

What you will be asked to do

You will be asked to make one or more hypothetical purchase decisions, followed by a short survey in the end. The study will take less than 10 minutes to complete.

Possible risks and discomforts

Minimal risks, which may include fatigue or eye strain, may occur. The risks or discomforts anticipated are not beyond what you may expect to experience in your daily lives.

Compensation/Reimbursement

You will receive $0.40 as the compensation of your participation in this study, plus a bonus up to a maximum of $0.40. Once you complete the study, you will receive your paycode. You MUST enter the paycode correctly in order to receive your compensation.

Confidentiality & Anonymity

Your responses are anonymous and thus results will be reported with no reference to you specifically. No information that could allow the researcher to identify you personally will be collected. Therefore, your responses cannot be linked to your identity. The MTurk system protects your privacy by making sure that you will only be identified to researchers with a unique numeric code that the system will assign, not by your name.

Once the study has been completed, all data files will be stored on a server at University College London for a period of 5 years, post-publication as per Erasmus University Rotterdam and University of College London policy.

Questions or Concerns

If you have any questions regarding the study, please contact the study administrator at ucznliu@ucl.ac.uk

Consent

Please click on the I AGREE button below, if you have understood to your satisfaction the information regarding participation in the research project, that you are aware that all records are entirely confidential and that you may discontinue participation at any point in the study, and that you agree to participate.
